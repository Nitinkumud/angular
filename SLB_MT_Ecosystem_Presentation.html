TLM (Maintenance)
TLM – Technology Lifecycle Management

A cross-division support function that manages all technologies from inception to retirement. Each business line hosts its own TLM organisation. MT is one of the key tools that supports TLM teams globally.

All divisions share something in common

Every division has shops and field sites where equipment must be maintained. This is why MT is relevant to all three business lines — not just one.

MT Basics

What is MT (Maintenance Tool)?

📋 Definition

MT = Maintenance Tool. It is SLB's proprietary ecosystem of applications designed to manage the maintenance lifecycle of field equipment — from pumps and engines to coiled tubing units and stimulation fleets.

🔗 Where MT Sits

📱 Field Apps / Mobile Users
↓
⚙️ MT Ecosystem (Intermediate Layer)
↓
🗄️ CMMS – Maximo (IBM) or SAP

🎯 Why MT Exists

  • Field locations are remote & offline-prone
  • Commercial CMMS apps aren't designed for rugged field use
  • Different business lines have different workflows
  • Need for real-time data streaming from equipment
  • Single entry point for all maintenance records before reaching CMMS

🏪 CMMS Split by Business Line

CMMSBusiness Lines
Maximo (IBM)Some divisions
SAPOther divisions

MT handles both — acting as a flexible intermediary regardless of which CMMS is used downstream.

Think of MT like this

Imagine every SLB field location is a restaurant chain. MT is the kitchen management system — it tracks all the equipment in every kitchen (restaurant), ensures preventive maintenance is scheduled, records defects, and reports upward to the corporate ERP (Maximo/SAP).

Products

MT – Two Core Offerings

📱 Product 1 — Mobile & Web Applications

Apps that help maintenance technicians and operators record, track, and manage equipment activities in the field.

Key App Functions

  • Record maintenance tasks performed
  • Report defective / inefficient equipment
  • Track preventive maintenance schedules (PM Cal)
  • View equipment health summaries
  • Plan interventions and repairs
  • Work offline — sync when connected
Offline-first Design

Because field sites can have no internet, apps connect to a local server (Mac Mini) on-site. Data syncs to cloud when connectivity is restored.

📡 Product 2 — Real-Time Data Streaming

Continuous streaming of operational data from equipment (pumps, engines) to SLB servers for monitoring and analysis by data science teams.

What Gets Streamed?

  • Pump pressure & RPM
  • Engine voltage & temperature
  • Operational hours (active / idle / offline)
  • Real-time equipment status signals
Business Value

Data science teams use streamed data to trigger early failure alerts — enabling predictive maintenance before equipment breaks down completely, reducing costly downtime and improving safety.

Architecture

Field Setup Architectures

MT supports multiple deployment architectures depending on the field scenario and connectivity available at the location.

🏭 Standard Shop / Remote Site
  • Mobile devices (tablets / phones)
  • Network equipment (Wi-Fi / LAN)
  • Local Server (Mac Mini) on-site
  • Apps talk only to local server
  • Local server syncs to cloud backend
Offline-capable Most common
🛢️ Offshore / Desert Well Sites
  • Same as standard + Satellite / Starlink
  • Or 4G / 5G depending on region
  • Internet is unreliable — local server is critical
  • Mac Mini buffers all data until stable connection
  • Flexible ISP support (Starlink, commercial 4G/5G)
Low connectivity Offline-first
🌐 Online-Only (Simple)
  • Mobile devices only
  • No local server required
  • Direct connection to internet / backend
  • Works like a standard commercial app
  • Limited offline capability
Online required Limited offline

Applications

MT Applications Overview

MT is not one app — it is an ecosystem of applications. The same apps can be embedded in different business line workflows. The goal is to consolidate apps over time to reduce end-user confusion.

📅 PM Cal (Preventive Maintenance Calendar)

Shows when preventive maintenance is due for each piece of equipment. Used by both operators and TLM technicians to plan and schedule work before failures occur.

All Business Lines

⚠️ Issues & Deficiency Reporting

Operators and technicians log equipment issues. Each issue is categorised as a Deferred Maintenance (not stopping operations) or Failed Asset (major failure requiring stop).

PSD + TLM

👥 Crew & People Management

Track how many people are on-site, their roles, and activity. Helps supervisors manage shift coverage and accountability for each field crew.

TLM Supervisors

📊 Real-Time Equipment Dashboard

Web dashboard summarising live status of all equipment at a crew location — showing active/idle/offline time, pump activity charts, and streaming data health.

Web App

📡 Data Streaming Monitor

Shows which equipment is actively streaming real-time data to SLB servers. Data science teams use this to monitor sensor feeds and detect early failure signals.

Data Team

🗂️ Asset Management

Full equipment profile — location, type, history, open issues, and service records. Provides a single source of truth for each piece of equipment across its full lifecycle.

Full Lifecycle
Ongoing Consolidation Initiative

Currently there are many apps across business lines with overlapping functions. A key goal for the team is to reduce and consolidate these into fewer, more powerful apps that cover multiple workflows — reducing confusion for end users.

Summary

Key Takeaways for New Joiners

1️⃣ MT is an Ecosystem

Not a single app — it's a suite of apps covering multiple workflows across multiple business lines. Each business line has slightly different workflows but uses the same core apps.

2️⃣ Architecture Varies by Location

Setup depends on the type of field location. Remote/offshore sites need a Mac Mini local server. Online-only sites connect directly. Know which architecture applies before supporting a deployment.

3️⃣ Offline-First is a Core Feature

Many SLB field sites are in deserts, offshore, or remote regions. The local server design means MT works without internet — a key differentiator over commercial solutions.

4️⃣ You Own Both App & Hardware

The team is responsible for developing apps AND supporting on-site hardware (Mac Minis, network gear). This makes you a full-stack field technology team, not just developers.

5️⃣ MT Bridges Field to CMMS

MT is the intermediate layer between field operations and enterprise CMMS (Maximo or SAP). All maintenance records flow through MT before reaching the corporate system of record.

6️⃣ Consolidation is Ongoing

The team is actively working to reduce the number of apps and consolidate workflows. Understanding which apps overlap across business lines is important for this initiative.
